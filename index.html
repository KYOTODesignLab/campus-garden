About · 03

On recording change.

This project treats a single campus garden as a specimen observed across time. Between two survey campaigns — early spring 2024 and late summer 2025 — the same ground was captured photographically and as a terrestrial laser scan, then registered into one coordinate frame.

Where conventional architectural drawing fixes a building at the instant of completion, a garden refuses that stillness. Foliage advances, a sapling thickens, a path is re-laid, a bench is removed. The aim here is not to arrest those changes but to make them legible — to let natural and human processes of growth and recession become part of the design language rather than noise to be cleaned away.

Method

Point clouds from the two epochs were aligned by ICP registration and differenced with the M3C2 algorithm, which measures signed distance along local surface normals. Positive distance (growth toward the sensor) reads warm; recession reads cool; stable surfaces fall out near zero. The same ramp colours every quantitative element on this page, so the eye learns one scale and reuses it.

Reading the record

The comparison view holds the two epochs against each other; the plates collect fixed vantage points; the 3D viewer lets you move freely through the differenced cloud. Together they describe a garden less as an object than as a relationship between two times.
